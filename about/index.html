About me

I am currently a Master’s student at the Instituto de Ciências Matemáticas e de Computação (ICMC) of the University of São Paulo.

My current research focuses on leveraging insights from the theory of computation to design sequence layers for neural networks that are both efficient and capable of strong generalization. Check out my publications here.

Academic: Prior to joining my current program, I completed my undergraduate studies at the Federal University of Juiz de Fora, where I conducted research as part of the Humunuculus Computing Group. There, I worked on the processing of brain signals using deep learning techniques.

Professional: Before transitioning into academic research, I worked as a full-stack web developer at Code Jr, and later served as the director of the department of project development.

Outside of work, reverse engineering and Rust programming have been among my long-standing interests.
